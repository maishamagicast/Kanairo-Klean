— What Is EPR?

Extended Producer Responsibility

Under the Sustainable Waste Management Act 2022, companies that manufacture or import packaged goods in Kenya are legally required to demonstrate that a proportional amount of that packaging is recovered and recycled.

The compliance burden falls on producers — but the evidence must come from the recovery chain. Without verified collection data, there is no compliance.

Key Deadline

Compliance obligations are active from January 2026. First NEMA audit cycle begins July 2026. Non-compliance penalties: up to KES 5M per quarter.

— Regulatory Timeline
Jan 2025

EPR Regulations Published

Kenya's NEMA publishes the Extended Producer Responsibility regulations under the Sustainable Waste Management Act 2022.

Jul 2025

Industry Consultation Period

Manufacturers, importers, and recyclers submit compliance roadmaps to NEMA. Documentation requirements clarified.

Jan 2026 →

Compliance Framework Active

EPR obligations take effect. Companies must demonstrate material recovery chains with verified documentation.

Jul 2026 →

First Annual Audit Cycle

NEMA conducts first compliance audits. Non-compliant entities face levies of up to KES 5M per quarter.
